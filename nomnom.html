NOM NOM

The food truck industry has grown rapidly in the past years and the mobile nature of the food trucks present unique needs for the truck owners and their customers. I believe that this problem can be solved by building a mobile app. Nom nom is an iOS app using which users can locate food trucks around them, find out about the services offered by them, and place their food order.

DELIVARABLE

iOS mobile app

SKILLS AND RESPONSIBILITY

UX Research, UX Design, Brand Identity

TOOLS AND SOFTWARES

Balsamiq, Sketch, Illustrator, Invision

THE CHALLENGE

EASY BROWSING AND
ORDERING EXPERIENCE

The food truck has grown exponentially over the last 5 years. This poses as a great opportunity and challenge to create a product which caters to provide an easy browsing processes and place an order. One of the notable challenges is to do this in real time and make the app easy and simple to understand.

RESEARCH

SURVEY FINDINGS

Users should be able to:
1. Explore food trucks in any area. They should have a way to track some of their favorite food trucks. 

2. Place an order remotely using the app so that they can only go to pick up their food and minimize their waiting time. 

3. Pay through the app itself.

IDENTITY AND BRANDING

DEFINING BRAND LANGUAGE

The app aims to be be trustworthy, warm and friendly. The logo is designed to be in shape of a food truck to enforce brand identity. The colors used evokes strong emotions and encourages appetite. Varela Round and Lato add to the simplicity.

WIREFRAMES

EXECUTION ON THE SCREENS

To prioritize which information/controls should be visible and to enhance usability, I conducted card sorting and then created low fidelity wireframes. I then created the high fidelity wireframes in Sketch. After this, I tested the designs and I got the feedback that though the interaction of the app seems simple enough, the visual design of lacked some appeal. I then went back analyzed the recent design trends and modified my wireframes. As a result, I reiterated and then created my final set of high fidelity wireframes

MAKING IT MORE USABLE

LESSONS LEARNT

I’m proud of the work I accomplished with Nom nom. It was a lot of fun learning the nuances of designing for iOS and creating an exclusively mobile experience for users. I think the biggest takeaway for me though was how much I grew by challenging myself to try new ideas and get uncomfortable in the process. From expanding my user research skills, to testing out new tools, to diving into the deep end of branding development, this experience has only strengthened that urge to jump into new challenges as a designer.
