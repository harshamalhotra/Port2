BAKSA

Baksa is a SaaS (Software as a Service) web application which allows people to collaborate with others in collecting and storing simple documents, images, and links to websites.

DELIVARABLE

Responsive Web App

SKILLS AND RESPONSIBILITY

UX Research, UX Design, Brand Identity

TOOLS AND SOFTWARE

Balsamiq, Sketch, Illustrator, Photoshop, Invision

THE CHALLENGE

A SIMPLE, RESPONSIVE WEB EXPERIENCE
FOR VARIETY OF CONTENT

To create a responsive web application which is simple and has an aesthetic appeal. It should also allow users to collaboratively create simple document, store images and save and store links to websites. We want to do this while making sure that the user experience is joyful.

COMPETITOR RESEARCH

SWOT ANALYSIS

I conducted a competitive analysis between Evernote, Google Keep and Things by using these products myself, by reading press articles about them and by reading their reviews in app store.
SWOT ANALYSIS

RESEARCH

SURVEY

I wanted to understand my potential users. So I created a survey and circulated it among my FB friends. The filter question filtered off those people who don't store their content on any software. I then asked the remaining people (about 10 in number) questions about how they were currently writing and saving their content. Some of the most important insights that I gained for the survey are as follows:
-Since many people were using different devices to access content, the app needed to be responsive.
-Available storage in free version was important.
-Current SaaS web solutions were complex and needed simplicity.
-Users wanted an auto save facility while writing to avoid information loss.
ANALYSIS OF THE RESULTS

PERSONA

UNDERSTANDING POTENTIAL USERS

Based on the surveys, it was clear that my potential user base had different and sometimes competing requirements. For instance, some power users cared about writing documents whereas most others cared about storing images. So I crafted user personas based on my research to ensure that I was designing for all the potential users.

IDENTITY AND BRANDING

DEFINING BRAND LANGUAGE

I wanted the users to think of the product as a box since it can store all their content. I played all around with various variations of the word “box” and decided to call it “baksa” since it sounds like box and is also the word for “box” in Hindi.
I designed three different logos — one with just a box and the other two with a “b” inside a box. I ended up choosing the one with just a box because I felt that it will inspire stronger brand recognition. To inspire trust and playfulness with professionalism, I decided to use Helvetica Neue font for the text for its mix of classic and modern. Garamond exudes professionalism and blue color exudes feelings of dependability and trust.

WIREFRAMES

EXECUTION ON THE SCREENS 

While sketching the hero page of Baksa, I created four versions of the hero page and conducted a preference test with 10 users. I then finally created the low fidelity wireframes using Balsamiq. After getting a fair idea about the structure, I then designed the medium fidelity wireframes in gray scale. After receiving a positive feedback on a usability test conducted on Peek, I created the high fidelity wireframes by adding color and content to the medium fidelity wireframes.

DEVELOPMENT

CODING WITH HTML AND CSS

I deployed baksa on Gitbhub after coding it using HTML and CSS. With the of use media queries, I made it responsive. This was a challenge and I eventually learnt Chrome's testing tools while figuring out how to test the design in different screen sizes.

USABILITY TESTING

MAKING IT MORE USABLE

I asked three people how they felt about baksa’s design. They really appreciated the use of bold colors and thought the site looked easy to use. They especially liked the display of the items on the dashboard.The users thought that they would like to have more functionality in the product. They also wanted the sign out option to be more visible.

LESSONS LEARNT

Baksa was a great learning experience for me. I used design tools like Balsamiq, Sketch, Photoshop, and Illustrator for the very first time. I employed various research methods and fused my results into the design process. I also learnt to write HTML and CSS along with the media queries. In addition, I became more comfortable the terminal and also learnt to version control my code using git and github. All in all, I learnt a lot while creating baksa and the confidence that I developed from working on baksa helped me hone my skills in future projects.
