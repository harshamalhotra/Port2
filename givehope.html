GIVE HOPE

Give Hope is a web-application that attempts to help kids who need help, all around the world. It allows people to raise funds by running crowd-funding fundraisers for children related causes (e.g. medical, education). It also allows people to browse and donate to any open fundraisers.

TOOLS AND SOFTWARE

THE CHALLENGE

A DELIGHTFUL WEB EXPERIENCE
FOR GENERATING GENEROSITY

The challenge was to create a product that would aim to create a sense of warmth and sympathy for the plight of the kids in the hearts of the people who wanted to help. It also had to provide a platform to the people who wanted to create a fundraiser and tell the story in the most effective way possible.

RESEARCH

SURVEY FINDINGS

1. Despite being comfortable with technology, people preferred donating in-person due to lack of trust on a website

2. Some respondents felt tracking mechanisms of projects were ineffective

3. Respondents were motivated to donate because of reasons like karma, recognition, happiness, and satisfaction. Overall, they felt good about helping some one in need.

INTERVIEW

INTERACTION WITH SEASONED FUNDRAISERS

After my interviews, it was found that the fundraisers initially started collected funds from everybody, but over the years found that a core group of their contacts collectively donated large sums for all the events. Another interesting observation was that many donors felt uncomfortable about other people finding out their donation amounts. Some also preferred to keep their donation anonymous.

My conclusion from this was that people should be able to donate anonymously and that it should also be possible to just hide their donation amount.

PERSONA

UNDERSTANDING POTENTIAL USERS

After my survey and interviews, it was clear that like most consumer products, Give Hope needed to cater to a wide variety of users. Some users wanted to donate and keep a track of the project. Another set of users wanted to create projects and find backers.

IDENTITY AND BRANDING

DEFINING BRAND LANGUAGE

The application exudes the feeling of trust and well-being. I chose orange, because the color is known to promote optimism, warmth and encouragement. Blue color is know to promote trust and professionalism.

WIREFRAMES

EXECUTION ON THE SCREENS 

After analyzing my research notes, I concluded that the action of donation should be the primary call of action. I then designed the low fidelity wireframes using Balsamiq. After getting some feedback , I then designed the medium fidelity wireframes and tried cutting some steps for completing a donation. After several iterations, I finally designed the high fidelity wireframes using color in Sketch.

LESSONS LEARNT

Give Hope definitely taught me a lot about the intricacies of raising funds for charity. However, the most important learning for me happened when I was trying to reduce the overhead of creating a fundraiser. This forced me to think deeply about the product and visual design choices. As part of this project, I also developed a deeper appreciation for the problem of fostering trust in online communities.
