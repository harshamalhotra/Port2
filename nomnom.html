NOM NOM

The food truck industry has grown rapidly in the past years and the mobile nature of the food trucks present unique needs for the truck owners and their customers. I believe that this problem can be solved by building a mobile app. Nom nom is an iOS app using which users can locate food trucks around them, find out about the services offered by them, and place their food order.

DELIVARABLE

iOS mobile app

SKILLS AND RESPONSIBILITY

UX Research, UX Design, Brand Identity

TOOLS AND SOFTWARES

Balsamiq, Sketch, Illustrator, Invision

THE CHALLENGE

EASY BROWSING AND
ORDERING EXPERIENCE

To design a design solution after understanding the use cases and the pain points of the customers of food trucks. The app was to be able to inspire user trust and should be delightful to use in additional to being functional. I learned to judge the usability of my design from user's perspective and the importance of involving users early on in the design process.

COMPETITOR RESEARCH

SWOT ANALYSIS

To understand the the market conditions and the products of the current players operating in the sphere, I did a SWOT analysis of three potential competitors - Yelp, Grubhub, and Roaming Hunger Food Truck Finder.

RESEARCH

SURVEY

I wanted to understand what the users were looking for in their food truck experience. To accompolish this, I asked 20 people about their food truck experience. Some of the findings were as follows: 

-Users should be able to explore food trucks in any area. They should have a way to track some of their favorite food trucks. 

-Users should be able to place an order remotely using the app so that they can only go to pick up their food and minimize their waiting time. 

-Users should be able to pay through the app itself.

DEFINING BRAND LANGUAGE

I wanted to make the app look approachable and trustworthy with a casual and fun tone. And so: 

1. I designed the logo to look like a food truck so that it establishes a clearer association with the target market of food trucks. 

2. The red color evokes strong emotions and encourages appetite and hence like most food apps, 

3. The Lato and Varela Round fonts were used to exude warmth as well as seriousness in the app.

WIREFRAMES

EXECUTION ON THE SCREENS

To prioritize which information/controls should be visible and to enhance usability, I conducted card sorting and then created low fidelity wireframes. I then created the high fidelity wireframes in Sketch. After this, I tested the designs and I got the feedback that though the interaction of the app seems simple enough, the visual design of lacked some appeal. I then went back analyzed the recent design trends and modified my wireframes. As a result, I reiterated and then created my final set of high fidelity wireframes
